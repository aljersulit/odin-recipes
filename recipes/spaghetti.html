Spaghetti Recipe

Classic Spaghetti with Meat Sauce

This classic spaghetti recipe is a simple yet flavorful dish featuring al dente pasta coated in a rich tomato-based meat sauce. The sauce is made by simmering ground beef, onions, garlic, and tomatoes with Italian herbs. Topped with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and served with a side of garlic bread, this meal is perfect for any occasion, whether a weeknight dinner or a cozy family gathering.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) spaghetti
  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (for serving)
  • Fresh basil or parsley (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Boil a large pot of salted water. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and saut√© until softened, about 3-5 minutes.
  3. Add the ground beef or sausage to the pan. Cook until browned, breaking it up into small pieces with a spoon. Drain any excess fat.
  4. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, pepper, and sugar (optional). Stir to combine and bring the sauce to a simmer.
  5. Let the sauce simmer for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and the flavors meld together.
  6. Once the sauce is ready, add the cooked spaghetti to the pan and toss to coat the pasta evenly in the sauce.
  7. Serve the spaghetti hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil or parsley if desired.
  8. Enjoy with garlic bread or a side salad for a complete meal!
